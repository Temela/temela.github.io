I’m **Fr. Anthony NDUKA**, a Catholic priest and early-career researcher working at the intersection of **data science, ethics, and human-centered computing**. My academic background in **Philosophy**, **Theology**, and **Information Technology** shapes a research outlook that’s both interdisciplinary and values-driven.

I currently serve as a **Graduate Assistant** in the **Department of Computer Science at** <a href='spiritanuniversity.edu.ng'>**Spiritan University Nneochi**</a>, and as **Director of the** <a href='spiritanhecap.org'>**Spiritan Healthcare Apostolate**</a>, where I apply data-driven approaches to support elderly care and healthcare outreach.

---

## Research Interests

- Human-Centered Computing  
- Responsible AI and Fairness  
- Health Informatics and Assistive Technologies  
- Ethics in Data Science and System Design  
- Civic Tech and Distributed Systems  

My recent work includes designing a **distributed biometric voting system** for INEC Nigeria, improving transparency and accessibility for displaced and physically challenged voters.

---

## Why I Research

My research begins with the human person. I’m interested in how intelligent systems can serve society without reinforcing bias, exclusion, or harm. As a priest, I approach technology from the standpoint of **human dignity** and **moral responsibility**. As a data scientist, I apply analytical tools and design thinking to build accountable, inclusive systems.

---

## Context and Commitment

My work spans both **academic and applied settings**. At the Spiritan Healthcare Apostolate, I lead initiatives focused on:

- Predictive health models for older adults  
- Tools for fall detection and mobility tracking  
- Technology outreach in underserved communities  

This dual context—research and practice—grounds my academic ambitions in real-world needs and impact.  

---

## Beyond Academia

Outside research and ministry, I’m a **photographer**, **graphic designer**, and occasional **badminton player**. I’ve led outreach efforts across Nigeria and Europe, organized healthcare drives for the elderly, and continue to advocate for digital equity rooted in empathy and justice.
